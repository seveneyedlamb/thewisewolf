**HOOK** How can a semi-automatic handgun with "dozens of rounds" be seen from across the street? The answer reveals why you should question the official story about Alex Pretti's death.

**CONTEXT** On Saturday, in Minneapolis, federal agents shot and killed Alex Pretti, a 37-year-old ICU nurse. The Department of Homeland Security claims he was a violent threat with a loaded firearm. But something doesn't add up. As believers, we must examine world events through the lens of Scripture, asking if these are signs of the times.

**MAIN ANALYSIS**

*Questions Surrounding the Shooting* On that fateful Saturday, Alex Pretti intervened when federal agents pepper-sprayed a woman at point-blank range. What followed was a brutal encounter where multiple agents surrounded him, beat him while he was on the ground, and shot him multiple times. He later died in the hospital.

*The Official Narrative* Secretary Kristi Noem described Pretti as approaching officers "menacingly with a semi-automatic handgun with dozens of rounds of ammunition." This claim is where things start to unravel. How exactly can one see "dozens of rounds" unless they were magically visible outside the gun? Unless Pretti was holding a firearm in one hand and a box of loose ammunition in the other, like he was at a shooting range, there's no way this claim holds water.

While this verse speaks of natural disasters, it also hints at social unrest and conflict. The shooting of Alex Pretti is just one example of the growing tension and mistrust between citizens and those in power.
