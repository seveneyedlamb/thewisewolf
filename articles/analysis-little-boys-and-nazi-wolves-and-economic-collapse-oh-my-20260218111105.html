The Boy Who Cried Wolf... and the Wolves at the Door You know the story - a shepherd boy gets bored watching his flock and starts yelling "Wolf! Wolf!" as a prank. When the villagers rush to help, they find no wolf, just a laughing boy. He does this a few more times, until one day, a real wolf shows up. The boy cries "Wolf!" but this time, no one believes him. The wolf attacks the sheep, and possibly the boy himself.

This timeless parable is not just a lesson about the dangers of lying. It's also a vivid metaphor for the way that genuine warnings can be dismissed or ignored, until it's too late. And in the context of end-times Bible prophecy, this parable takes on an ominous new significance.

Wolves in Sheep's Clothing Just as the villagers failed to heed the shepherd boy's real warning, many believers today are failing to recognize the prophetic "wolves" that are stalking the flock. From the global economic crisis to the erosion of religious freedoms, the signs of the times are all around us. Yet all too often, these warnings are met with skepticism, ridicule, or indifference.

The Scriptures are clear that in the last days, "wolves in sheep's clothing" will arise, seeking to deceive even the elect (Matthew 7:15). These false prophets and teachers will use cunning lies and half-truths to lead believers astray. And just as the villagers ignored the boy's cries, many Christians are dismissing the urgent warnings of modern-day watchmen and prophets.

A Time of Testing But make no mistake - the "wolf" is real, and it's drawing ever closer. The global elites are openly pushing for a "Great Reset" of the world order, one that would strip away our freedoms and religious liberties. Economically, the stage is set for a collapse that could make the Great Depression look like a mild recession. And geopolitically, the world is on a collision course with the final prophetic events of the end times.

These are not the paranoid ravings of a doomsday cult. These are sober assessments based on a clear-eyed look at current events through the lens of biblical prophecy. Just as the villagers learned the hard way, turning a deaf ear to genuine warnings can have devastating consequences. The only question is: Will the church heed the cry of the watchmen, or will we too become victims of the wolves at the door?

"The truth doesn't hide. It waits for those brave enough to look."
